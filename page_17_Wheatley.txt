To MAECENAS

MAECENAS, you, beneath the myrtle
shade,
Read o’er what poets fung, and shepherds play’d,
What felt those poets but you feel the same?
Does not your foul possess the sacred flame?
Their noble strains your equal genius shares 5
In softer language, and diviner airs.

While Homer paints lo! circumfus’d in air,

Celestial Gods in mortal forms appear;
B Swift
